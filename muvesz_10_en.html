The bequest of György Leszkovszky (1891-1968)

The donation of Ágnes Öcsényi Andrásné Leszkovszky,
Márta Neszmélyi Andrásné Leszkovszky
and Pál P. Leszkovszky OP


The unjustly forgotten painter, who was a significant though less recognized figure of Christian art between 1910-1960, began his studies as a student of Aladár Körösfői-Kriesch. With the foundation of the Cennini Society in 1920, that put the ideal humble artist of medieval handcraft traditions' on its flag, he emphasized his respect for his master, however finally he moved away from the pantheistic inclinations of the master and his circle in Gödöllő, and turned to religious painting which befitted better his profound religiosity. Leszkovszky, although he also won awards as a sculptor and as a watercolour painter, was mainly a mural artist. His works of various mural techniques include the plans of Zebegény church frescoes (1910-14), the wall paintings of the chapel of St. János Hospital (1927-33) and the Cathedral of Kaposvár (1935-1943). In addition to his genuine mural works and glass window designs he also restored wall paintings. Besides his practical activities he wrote theoretical treatises about the fresco, secco and sgraffito techniques. His precisely elaborated church interior painting plans and stained glass window designs are under the influence of the style of the Gödöllő artists' colony, the Pre-Raphaelites and the Nazarenes.

The Leszkovszky bequest in the Gödöllő Town Museum's collection mainly consists of pencil and watercolour sketches, but the broad range of artworks include stained glass window designs and fresco plans, oil paintings, ceiling tiles and a dome model as well. In addition to György Leszkovszky's artworks the artist's letters, photographs and official documents can also be found in the bequest.
