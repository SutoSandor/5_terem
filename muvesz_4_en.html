Monumental art


The Gödöllő artists' wish to make everyday life and environment more beautiful, the desire to teach all the people appeared in their monumental art as well. They deemed the decoration of buildings important because it is visible for the people on the street not just for those who visit galleries.

The still existing works that can be related to the artists working by the intellectual leadership of Aladár Körösfői-Kriesch, such as Sándor Nagy, Mariska Undi and István Zichy are: the frescos of the Parliament (1898), the frescoes of the Academy of Music (1907), the glass window and facade sgraffito of the Petőfi Theatre in Veszprém (1907); the mosaics of the Hungarian Pavilion in Venice (1909), the paintings in the dome of the Kerepesi cemetery's sepulchral vault (1909), in Budapest Székesfőváros Népszálló (Budapest Capital Public Hotel, 1911), the glass paintings of the City Hall in Subotica (1912), the glass paintings in the Lipótmező chapel (1913) and the Cultural Palace in Tirgu Mures (1911˗13) which is the joint work of the artists of the Gödöllő artists' colony. Aladár Körösfői-Kriesch created the paintings in Zebegény church, the wall painting of the Theatre Academy's Egressy hall (1916) and the frescoes of the Mester Street Business School in Budapest (1917) together with his students. His last known work was the glass painting entitled the Good Shepherd (1918) for the Seminary in Budapest.

To create mural art: fresco, secco, sgraffito, mosaic art or stained glass requires precise preparation. The artist first realizes his or her idea in a colored sketch, then, if the work is in a dome or on other curved surfaces, he or she creates a model as well. Afterwards it is necessary to make a full-scale cartoon - the same size as the work of art -, which is also present at the execution of the work of art. Before the final painting the cartoon's outlines are copied to tracing paper, which is then perforated and through these small holes, with the help of coal dust, the outlines are transferred to the wall surface.

In our exhibition we try to present the still existing monumental works of the Gödöllő artists, mainly by our collection of coloured sketches and cartoons, and a dome model.
